TeamTrack: An Algorithm and Benchmark Dataset for Multi-Sport Multi-Object Tracking in Full-pitch Videos

The TeamTrack dataset captures the full-swing action of soccer, basketball, and handball games on full-pitch, with high-resolution fisheye and drone cameras, producing a stunning visual treat. With a resolution ranging from 4K to 8K, it includes a colossal amount of over 4 million annotated bounding boxes, providing an unprecedented resource for developing MOT algorithms.

Abstract

This paper presents TeamTrack, a new benchmark dataset and algorithm for multi-object tracking (MOT) in team sports, specifically in full-pitch videos. MOT in team sports is a challenging task due to object occlusions, similar appearances, and complex movements. Existing methods often struggle to accurately track objects in these scenarios. To address this challenge, the proposed TeamTrack dataset captures diverse object appearances and movements in soccer, basketball, and handball games using high-resolution fisheye and drone cameras. The dataset includes over 4 million annotated bounding boxes and provides a comprehensive resource for developing and evaluating MOT algorithms. The paper also introduces a new MOT approach that incorporates trajectory forecasting using a graph neural network (GNN) to model complex group movement patterns. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm on the TeamTrack dataset.

Tracking Algorithm

The TeamTrack Algorithm is a state-of-the-art method designed for tracking multiple objects in sports scenarios. This innovative algorithm uniquely combines object detection, trajectory forecasting, and data association to accurately maintain identities of various entities, even within dynamic and complex sports environments.

  • Object Detection Model:

    The algorithm employs a fine-tuned YOLOv8 model for object detection, renowned for its ability to detect human objects effectively. This customized model enhances its capability to differentiate between players, supporters, and referees.

  • Trajectory Forecasting Model:

    TeamTrack Algorithm uses a Graph Neural Network (GNN) for trajectory forecasting. This model simulates the interactions between multiple agents and introduces an element of randomness. The algorithm also employs the Kalman filter to smooth the projected trajectories.

  • Data Association Model:

    This part of the algorithm draws inspiration from both DeepSORT and ByteTrack, known for handling temporarily occluded or out-of-frame objects, and predicting objects' positions in tracklets for the next frame, respectively.

A distinguishing feature of the TeamTrack Algorithm is its use of a bird's-eye view (BEV) and homography transformation, translating the bottom-center points of detected object boundaries to 2D BEV coordinates. This approach allows effective tracking, given the static camera's ability to capture the entire field and the complex interactions of the players.
